What is Nuclear Engineering?

Nuclear Engineering came with the emergence of Nuclear fission technology in 1939. Nuclear Engineering is the research and development of methods to utilize nuclear energy, radiation, and waste management for the generation of power/energy, medical systems and diagnostics, and industrial applications. Some of the core responsibilities and application of a Nuclear Engineer is to design, operate, and manage Nuclear reactors in Nuclear power plants. Furthermore, Nuclear Engineers also innovate medical technology through the development of imaging techniques and treatments. To become a Nuclear Engineer, you need a Bachelor's degree in Nuclear Engineering or Physics/Math related fields of study. A strong foundation in Math and Science also key to success in the Nuclear Engineering field and industry.
