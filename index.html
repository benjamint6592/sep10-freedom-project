What is Nuclear Engineering?

Nuclear Engineering came with the emergence of Nuclear fission technology in 1939. Nuclear Engineering is the research and development of methods to utilize nuclear energy, radiation, and waste management for the generation of power/energy, medical systems and diagnostics, and industrial applications. Some of the core responsibilities and application of a Nuclear Engineer is to design, operate, and manage Nuclear reactors in Nuclear power plants. Furthermore, Nuclear Engineers also innovate medical technology through the development of imaging techniques and treatments. To become a Nuclear Engineer, you need a Bachelor's degree in Nuclear Engineering or Physics/Math related fields of study. A strong foundation in Math and Science also key to success in the Nuclear Engineering field and industry.

Current & Widely Used Nuclear Technology by Engineers

Simulating Nuclear Energy

CASMO5 models Pressurized Water Reactor and Boiling Water Reactor fuel with very high accuracy, innovating the nuclear scene. It uses Method of Characteristics for fast and precise 2D transport solutions, uses physics models for reliable testing, and integrates previous software for reactor design and fuel management.

Extra: Requires a dual-core processor with 4GB available memory.

New Nuclear Hardware

The Department of Energy is working toward moving 11 advanced nuclear reactor projects toward deployment and application. This allows nuclear engineers to work in a safer environment and progress nuclear reactor hardware. The goal is to test at least 3 reactors with DOE authorization by 2026.

This represents a push for advanced nuclear energy hardware outside of laboratory testing and a cleaner energy future.

Emerging Research & Applied Physics in Innovation

Current-Gen Nuclear Engineering and New Innovation

New scientists in the nuclear industry are working toward new designs and technology. Researchers at Argonne National Laboratory are focusing on advanced reactor design and safety analysis, materials management, reactor and fuel cycle physics, modeling and simulation, and sensors and diagnostics.

Extra: Uses top-of-the-line supercomputers and advanced AI systems.

How Applied Physics is Used in Nuclear Engineering

Applied Physics connects physics and engineering to create practical solutions to real-world problems. It drives innovation in renewable energy technology and supports nuclear engineering through analytical, computational, and experimental skills.
